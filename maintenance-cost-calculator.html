Definition

Maintenance and support cost is the recurring monthly budget to keep a Shopify store healthy, secure, and improving — covering fixes, updates, app management, and optimization.

What is the maintenance cost calculator?

This free tool estimates a sensible monthly Shopify support retainer. Rather than guessing, you describe your store size, the support level you want, the services to include, and your response-time needs, and get a realistic monthly range.

How this calculator works

It starts from a base cost set by store size, scales it by support level, adds for each included service, and adds for faster SLAs. The result is shown as a monthly range with an approximate hours figure and an annualised total.

How to read your result

The headline is your estimated monthly retainer. The rows show approximate hours, your support level, response time, and the annual equivalent. Retainers buy capacity — more hours mean more gets shipped each month.

When should you talk to a partner?

The right retainer depends on how much you want to ship versus simply keep the lights on. Talk to our support team to size a plan to your roadmap.

Shopify maintenance cost per month

Searches for "Shopify maintenance cost" and "Shopify support retainer" reflect a real planning need. A small store needing occasional fixes might spend $300–$800/month. A growing store wanting regular updates and improvements typically runs $800–$2,500/month, while a high-volume or Shopify Plus store with proactive optimization and fast response can exceed $3,500/month. The Shopify maintenance cost scales with store size, the services included, and how quickly you need a response.

A good retainer is not just break-fix. The Shopify maintenance cost buys monthly capacity to ship improvements — CRO, content, performance, and security — so your store keeps getting better rather than simply staying online.

Choosing the right Shopify maintenance plan

Match your Shopify maintenance cost to your roadmap. If you mainly need stability, a lean essential retainer covers updates, security, and monitoring. If you want to grow, a higher tier funds a steady stream of improvements. Bundling maintenance with the team that knows your store reduces ramp-up and usually delivers better value than ad-hoc fixes, which makes the recurring Shopify maintenance cost easier to justify.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Shopify maintenance cost per month?

From a few hundred dollars for a small store needing occasional fixes to several thousand for a large store wanting proactive optimization and fast response. The drivers are size, support level, services, and SLA.

What does a maintenance retainer include?

Typically theme and app updates, bug fixes, security and backups, monitoring, and — at higher levels — CRO, content, and performance work. You choose the scope, which sets the cost.

Do I need a retainer or pay-as-you-go?

A retainer suits stores that need regular work and fast response; pay-as-you-go can work for very stable stores with rare needs. A retainer usually offers better rates and priority.

How many hours are in a retainer?

It varies by budget; this tool shows an approximate monthly hours figure for your selections. More hours mean more improvements shipped each month.

Does store size really change the cost?

Yes. Higher-volume stores carry more risk and more moving parts, so they need more monitoring and faster response, which raises the retainer.

Is the estimate a fixed quote?

No. It is an indicative monthly range. A precise retainer follows a short scoping conversation about your store and goals.
