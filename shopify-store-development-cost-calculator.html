Definition

Shopify store development cost is the estimated budget to design, build, configure, test, and launch an online store on Shopify or Shopify Plus, plus any migration and ongoing support.

What is the Shopify store development cost calculator?

This free tool turns a few simple choices about your store into a realistic price range, timeline, and complexity score. How this calculator works

The tool adds up the cost drivers that genuinely move the price of a Shopify build, then applies a confidence band to produce a low and high figure. Your store type sets the base build; design level scales from light theme work to a fully headless storefront; catalogue size, features, and integrations each add weighted cost; and migration adds data and SEO-preservation work. Support is shown separately as a monthly figure. The complexity score is a weighted read of all those choices and drives the suggested engagement model.

The timeline is calendar time to launch assuming content is reasonably ready. The complexity score shows how much specialist involvement the project needs. The suggested engagement maps your project to the way it is usually best delivered.

Shopify store development cost by store type

Search data shows most people asking "how much does it cost to build a Shopify store" fall into three brackets. A DIY or lightly customised store on a premium theme typically lands around $500–$3,000. A professionally built, custom-designed store usually costs $3,000–$15,000, while an enterprise or Shopify Plus build with custom features, integrations, and a bespoke front end commonly runs $15,000–$50,000+. These Shopify store development cost ranges reflect the developer rates, design depth, and functionality you choose rather than the platform fee, which is billed separately each month.

The biggest swing factor in any Shopify development cost is customisation. A customised theme is far cheaper than a fully bespoke or headless storefront, and every extra feature — subscriptions, bundles, a product configurator, or a custom checkout on Plus — adds scoped development time. Catalogue size, third-party app and ERP/CRM integrations, and migration from another platform all push the Shopify website development cost upward too.

How to control your Shopify development cost

You can keep your Shopify store development cost predictable without cutting corners. Start with a clear scope and an MVP feature set, then phase advanced functionality after launch once revenue justifies it. Choose a premium theme as a foundation where a fully custom design is not essential, reuse proven app integrations instead of custom-building everything, and prioritise the conversion features that pay back fastest. A specialist Shopify development partner can map this trade-off for you, so the money you spend on Shopify store development goes to the work that actually moves revenue.

Factors

Store type and business model

D2C, B2B, and enterprise builds differ in architecture, permissions, and checkout — the biggest driver of base cost.

Design and UX depth

A customized theme is far quicker than bespoke or headless front ends with custom interactions.

Number of products

Larger catalogues add data, variant logic, and performance work past a few thousand SKUs.

Features and functionality

Subscriptions, bundles, configurators, and custom checkout each add scoped development.

Third-party integrations

ERP, CRM, 3PL, and marketing systems add API work, data mapping, and sync logic.

Migration needs

Moving platforms adds data migration, redirects, and SEO preservation; Magento costs most.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to build a Shopify store?

A clean standard storefront usually starts in the low thousands, while custom, B2B, headless, or migration-heavy builds commonly run into the tens of thousands. The exact figure depends on design depth, catalogue size, features, integrations, and migration. This calculator gives an instant range tailored to your choices.

Is the estimate a fixed quote?

No. It is an indicative planning range based on typical project costs, not a contractual quote. Final pricing comes after a discovery conversation where scope, edge cases, and requirements are confirmed.

What makes a Shopify project more expensive?

Complex models like B2B or enterprise, bespoke or headless design, large catalogues, custom features, multiple integrations, and migration from platforms like Magento. Each adds development, testing, and project-management time.

How long does it take to build a Shopify store?

A focused standard build can launch in a few weeks; custom, integration-heavy, or migration projects typically take two to four months or more, assuming content is ready at kickoff.

Do I need ongoing support after launch?

Most growing stores benefit from a retainer for updates, app management, performance, and optimization. It is a recurring monthly cost shown separately from the build budget.
