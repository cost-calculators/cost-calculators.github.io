Shopify migration cost by source platform

The Shopify migration cost depends heavily on where you are migrating from. A WooCommerce to Shopify migration typically costs $3,000–$15,000 with an agency, because the data move is simple but replacing your plugin stack with Shopify apps takes work. A BigCommerce to Shopify migration is usually similar, with variant limits the main friction. A Magento (Adobe Commerce) to Shopify migration is the most expensive, commonly $20,000–$75,000+, driven by complex URLs, ERP integrations, and years of data debt. Small Wix or Squarespace moves can be far cheaper, often under $5,000.

As migration specialists repeatedly note, most of the cost to migrate to Shopify is not the data transfer itself — it is the theme rebuild, integration rewiring, and SEO preservation. Skipping proper 301 redirect mapping is the single most common cause of a 20–50% drop in organic traffic after a replatform, which is why a careful Shopify migration treats SEO as a core line item, not an afterthought.

Reducing risk in your Shopify migration

Lower your Shopify migration cost and risk by auditing first: list every product, plugin, integration, and custom workflow so quotes are accurate and nothing breaks on launch day. Clean your data before the move — orphaned categories and duplicate records inflate effort. Budget for the password-reset email every migrated store needs, and never migrate inside 90 days of peak season. A trusted Shopify migration partner will give you a redirect map and an SEO migration checklist up front, so your switch to Shopify protects the rankings and revenue you have already earned.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a Shopify migration cost?

Light WooCommerce migrations can start in the low thousands, while large Magento or custom replatforms with heavy data and many integrations run into the tens of thousands. The main drivers are source platform, catalogue and data volume, design, integrations, and SEO scope. This calculator gives an instant range for your situation.

Will I lose my SEO rankings when migrating to Shopify?

Not if migration is handled properly. A full SEO migration maps URL redirects, preserves metadata and structure, and protects internal links so rankings carry over. Skipping this is the most common cause of post-migration traffic drops, which is why the calculator treats SEO preservation as its own cost factor.

How long does a Shopify migration take?

A straightforward migration can complete in a few weeks; large catalogues, heavy order history, multiple integrations, or a full redesign push it to two to four months or more. Data migration and testing usually take longer than the design work.

Which platforms can I migrate from?

Commonly WooCommerce, Magento or Adobe Commerce, BigCommerce, and custom or WordPress builds. WooCommerce tends to be lightest and Magento the heaviest because of its data model. The calculator adjusts the estimate based on the platform you select.

Is the migration estimate a fixed quote?

No. It is an indicative planning range based on typical migration costs. A precise quote follows a discovery review of your data, integrations, and redirect map, where edge cases are confirmed.

Do I need ongoing support after migrating?

A retainer is recommended for the weeks after launch to monitor redirects, fix edge cases, and tune performance. It is a recurring monthly cost shown separately from the one-time migration budget.
