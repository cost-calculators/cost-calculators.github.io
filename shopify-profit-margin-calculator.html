Definition

Net profit margin is the percentage of each sale you keep after every cost - product, shipping, payment fees, and marketing - not just product cost.

What is the Shopify Profit Margin Calculator?

This free Shopify profit margin calculator reveals your real profit per order. Most store owners look only at gross margin and miss payment fees, shipping, and ad costs. This tool factors them all in to show your true net profit, net margin, and markup.

How this calculator works

Enter your selling price, product cost, per-order shipping, the Shopify payment fee percentage, and any other per-order cost such as ads or packaging. The calculator subtracts every cost from revenue to compute net profit, then expresses it as a margin and a markup.

How to read your result

The headline is your net profit per order. The rows show revenue, total cost, the payment fee, your net margin, and your markup. Most healthy stores aim for a net margin around 10 to 20 percent; if yours is thin, look at pricing, AOV, and conversion before cutting product quality.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good profit margin for a Shopify store?

Most successful stores run a net margin of roughly 10 to 20 percent after all costs. Above 20 percent is strong; below 10 percent is risky because there is little room for ad spend or surprises.

What is the difference between margin and markup?

Margin is profit as a percentage of the selling price; markup is profit as a percentage of cost. A 60 percent margin equals a 150 percent markup. This tool shows both.

Does this include Shopify fees?

Yes. Enter your payment fee percentage and the tool includes processing plus the fixed per-order fee in your net profit.
