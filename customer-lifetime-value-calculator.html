Definition

Customer lifetime value (LTV) is the total profit a typical customer generates across their whole relationship with your store.

What is the Customer Lifetime Value Calculator?

This free customer lifetime value calculator estimates the profit a typical customer brings over their lifetime, and the maximum you can spend to acquire one. LTV is the single most important number for deciding how aggressively you can invest in growth.

How this calculator works

Enter your average order value, how many times a customer buys per year, how many years they stay, and your gross margin. The tool multiplies these to get revenue LTV, applies your margin for profit LTV, and divides profit LTV by three to suggest a healthy maximum CAC.

How to read your result

The headline is your profit LTV per customer. The rows show revenue LTV, annual value, profit LTV, and the most you can spend to acquire a customer at a 3:1 ratio. If LTV is low, retention, subscriptions, and loyalty are the fastest ways to raise it.

Frequently asked questions

How is customer lifetime value calculated?

Multiply average order value by purchase frequency and customer lifespan for revenue LTV, then multiply by gross margin for profit LTV. This calculator does both and suggests a healthy maximum acquisition cost.

What is a good LTV:CAC ratio?

Around 3:1 is the widely used benchmark, three dollars of lifetime profit for every dollar of acquisition cost. Below 2:1 is usually unsustainable; far above 5:1 can mean you are under-investing in growth.

How do I increase LTV?

Improve retention with great post-purchase experience, email and SMS flows, loyalty programs, and subscriptions. Raising repeat purchase rate moves LTV more than chasing new traffic.
