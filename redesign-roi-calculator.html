Definition

Redesign ROI is the return on a store redesign — the extra revenue from a higher conversion rate, measured against the redesign cost and the time to recoup it.

What is the redesign ROI calculator?

This free tool turns a planned redesign into a revenue and payback estimate. Instead of treating a redesign as a pure cost, it shows the upside if the new design lifts conversion, so you can judge whether the project is worth it.

How this calculator works

It compares your current monthly revenue with revenue at a higher conversion rate after redesign, using your expected uplift. The monthly difference, annualised, is your gain; set against the project cost it produces a payback period and first-year ROI.

How to read your result

The headline is your projected annual revenue gain. The rows show before and after monthly revenue, the monthly gain, how fast the redesign pays back, and the first-year return. A realistic uplift is often in the range of a fifth to a third for an outdated store, but use a number you can defend.

When should you talk to a partner?

If the annual gain comfortably clears the cost, a redesign is easy to justify — and a conversion-led team will protect what already works while improving the rest. Get a free store review to pressure-test your numbers.

When a Shopify redesign pays off

Brands researching "Shopify redesign ROI" or "is a store redesign worth it" want to know whether the project returns more than it costs. The Shopify redesign ROI is strongest when your current store is dated, slow, or converting below its category average, because the conversion headroom is larger. A redesign that lifts conversion even modestly, applied across your existing traffic and average order value, can return its cost within months — which is exactly what this redesign ROI calculator models.

Use a defensible uplift figure. Outdated stores often see larger gains from a redesign than already-optimised ones, so base your number on analytics, heatmaps, or past tests rather than optimism.

Redesign or CRO first?

If your store is structurally sound, a focused CRO programme may deliver better redesign ROI per dollar than a full rebuild. If the design itself is the bottleneck — confusing navigation, weak mobile experience, slow pages — a redesign unlocks gains testing alone cannot. The best Shopify redesign ROI usually comes from pairing premium design with conversion strategy, so the new store is measured against revenue, not aesthetics.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate redesign ROI?

Estimate the conversion uplift the redesign will deliver, multiply the resulting revenue increase across your traffic and order value, annualise it, then compare to the project cost. This calculator does it instantly.

What conversion uplift is realistic from a redesign?

It varies, but outdated stores often see meaningful gains while already-strong stores see less. Use evidence from analytics or past tests rather than a hopeful number.

Is a redesign worth it for my store?

If the projected annual gain clears the redesign cost with margin and pays back within a few months, the case is usually strong. If it barely breaks even, a targeted CRO sprint may be a better first step.

How long until a redesign pays back?

The payback figure divides the project cost by the monthly gain. Many well-scoped redesigns pay back within a few months once the new design is live.

Should I redesign or just run CRO?

CRO improves your existing design through testing and is lower-risk; a redesign is bigger but can unseat structural problems. The CRO ROI calculator helps compare the lighter path.

Is this a guaranteed result?

No. It is a projection based on your inputs and assumes the expected uplift is achieved and sustained. Treat it as a planning estimate.
